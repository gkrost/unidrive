# Project name

UniDrive (also rendered "unidrive") is the project name. 

## Use in your projects

Apache 2.0 ([LICENSE](LICENSE)) governs the source code. 
You may use the name according to local and/or german law.

## Apache 2.0 and proprietary derivatives

Apache 2.0 explicitly permits **proprietary derivative works**,
including closed-source commercial software (mobile apps, SaaS
services, hardware appliances, paid Pro extensions). The unidrive
source you incorporate must keep its copyright notice and the
[LICENSE](LICENSE) + [NOTICE](NOTICE) files (Apache 2.0 §4); the rest
of your derivative work can be under any license — proprietary
included. This is the same model Cryptomator, Mountain Duck, and many
other open-core projects use.

The author is also building a non-OSS Android app ("Logic Arts
UniDrive" branding likely) on top of this codebase. Apache 2.0's
permissive grant explicitly allows that, and contributors who sign
off (`git commit -s`) on their patches understand that those patches
are licensed permissively to anyone — including for proprietary use.

## Attribution (required by Apache 2.0)

If you redistribute UniDrive source or binaries, retain the copyright
notice, the LICENSE file, and the NOTICE file. This is required by
Apache 2.0 §4 and is the only binding obligation the license imposes.

## Future trademark filing

If commercial demand for UniDrive justifies a Markenamt filing the author may register "UniDrive", "Logic Arts UniDrive"
in Germany. If that happens:

- This file will be updated to reflect the new status.
- Existing forks and derivative works will have a transition window
  (at minimum 90 days) to rebrand if they wish — but per the previous
  paragraphs you are not required to rebrand if your use predates the
  filing.
- Any registered mark would cover software goods/services only.
